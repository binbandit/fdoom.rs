A Fossicker's Guide

Your first day: punch tall grass for fibers, batter a tree for sticks, and pick up stones. Three fibers twist into cord; two stones knap into a sharp edge. A stick, a cord, and a sharp stone make your first tools.

Going down: there are no stairs in this world. Shovel a pit into open dirt and keep digging - the pit deepens until it strikes rock. Break through with a pickaxe and the floor gives way to the mines. The climb back waits where you fell.

The mines run three layers deep - iron, then gold, then gems in the lava dark. Bring light: the dark down there is real. Deeper still, behind obsidian gates in the third layer, an old dungeon sleeps.

The land itself: every biome feeds you differently - berries and orchards in the green country, mushrooms in the woods and caves, fruit off the desert cacti, coconuts on the shore. The tide breathes twice a day; comb the flats at low water.

Nights are dangerous, and some nights are wrong. Watch the dusk sky: a still evening, a warm glow, or shimmering lights each mean something is coming. Old trails link the ruins of those who came before - walk them, and mind the graves.
