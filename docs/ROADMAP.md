# Design Roadmap — "bountiful, mystical, fun to explore"

User mandate: open sandbox in the Minecraft spirit (never 1:1 copies), survival-crafting
depth in the Rust/7DtD spirit, creative freedom granted. Original designs only.

## Rare world events (deterministic per seed+day, announced by subtle cues first)
- **Ember Rain** — a night meteor shower; smoldering craters land in loaded chunks,
  rich in ore and char, cooling over days. Cue: the sky tints warm at dusk.
- **Hollow Night** — every cracked grave in loaded cemeteries opens at once; survive
  till dawn for a "quiet week" (no grave decay). Cue: no ambient mobs the evening before.
- **Whisper Fog** — dense fog banks roll over marshes; wisps swarm inside, and a
  one-night-only fog vault (loot room) surfaces somewhere in the bank.
- **Aurora** — tundra nights: hostile mobs docile, ice crystallizes into harvestable
  gem-ice. Cue: the map edge shimmers.
- **The Caravan** — ruined trader wagons appear on a trail; first player to walk the
  trail end-to-end that day finds the cargo intact.

## Mystical layer
- **Leylines**: faint worn paths link standing-stone rings; following a complete circuit
  reveals a buried vault (dig-descent entrance) with unique loot.
- **Depth shrines** in the mines: small carved rooms with an offering slot (trade
  materials for blessings — short potion-like effects).

## Bountiful layer
- Berry bushes (seasonal regrowth), fishing schools (visible ripples = better catches),
  beehives in forests (honey = food + crafting), firefly swarms at dusk (ambience).

## Principles
Deterministic per seed; cues before spectacle; everything findable by exploring, nothing
gated behind menus; loot feeds the crafting chains; no verbatim mechanics from other games.
